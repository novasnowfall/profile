Discovery

New Lumen Deep Field IV, NOVA-727 is located within the gravitationally distorted region towards the centre of the image

Visible spectrogram of NOVA-727 (380nm - 780nm)

25th September 2020

After the release of New Lumen Deep Field IV (01h56m45.3s, +33°03'30"), new discoveries have come to light within the constellation of Triangulum. Further analysis of the region has revealed galaxies with distances ranging between z = 0.6 and z = 2.5.

Despite these observations, a fairly bright and compact emission (designated NOVA-727) has been found inside a local galaxy with an anomalous spectral distribution and highly peculiar motion.

9th April 2025

Recent reports have observed NOVA-727's spectrum shifting. This research has found that spectral lines have been blue-shifted, implying a sudden and rapid acceleration towards Earth. It is projected to reach reach us within hmmmmmmm not yet.

The stunning speed of this phenomenon is yet to be understood, whilst the universe is no stranger to wandering objects, one moving at this velocity is highly unusual.
